SEASONAL WEATHER ADVISORY AND AGRICULTURAL RISK MANAGEMENT — INDIA 2025-2026

SECTION: KHARIF SEASON (June - October)
SOUTHWEST MONSOON ONSET: Normal onset dates — Kerala: June 1, Karnataka: June 5, Andhra Pradesh/Telangana: June 10-15, Maharashtra: June 10, Central India: June 15-20, North India: June 25-July 1.
RAINFALL DISTRIBUTION: Southwest monsoon contributes 70-80% of annual rainfall. IMD forecasts NORMAL monsoon (96-104% of Long Period Average) for 2025-26.
RECOMMENDED SOWING WINDOW: Begin sowing only after receiving 50-75mm of rainfall to ensure adequate soil moisture for germination. For paddy transplanting, ensure standing water of 2-5 cm in the field.

ADVISORY: ANDHRA PRADESH — KHARIF
Districts: Guntur, Krishna, Prakasam, Nellore
Expected Rainfall: 600-900 mm during June-September
Flood Risk: HIGH in Krishna and Godavari delta regions during August-September. Low-lying paddy fields prone to waterlogging.
Cyclone Risk: MODERATE — Bay of Bengal cyclonic activity peaks October-November. Coastal districts should complete paddy harvest before mid-October.
Recommendations: Use short-duration paddy varieties (BPT-5204, MTU-1010) that mature before cyclone season. Ensure drainage channels are clear before monsoon onset. Insure crops under PMFBY before the deadline.

ADVISORY: TELANGANA — KHARIF
Districts: Warangal, Karimnagar, Nizamabad, Nalgonda
Expected Rainfall: 700-1000 mm during June-September
Flood Risk: MODERATE — Godavari river flooding possible in Warangal and Karimnagar during heavy monsoon surges.
Drought Risk: LOW to MODERATE — Dry spells of 7-10 days possible in July (monsoon break period). Ensure supplemental irrigation availability.
Recommendations: For cotton, avoid sowing before June 15 to prevent early pest build-up. Use Bt cotton hybrids approved by GEAC. For maize, select medium-duration varieties and apply split nitrogen fertilization.

ADVISORY: TAMIL NADU — NORTHEAST MONSOON (October - December)
Districts: Thanjavur, Nagapattinam, Cuddalore, Chennai
Expected Rainfall: 400-600 mm during October-December
Cyclone Risk: HIGH — Northeast monsoon season coincides with Bay of Bengal cyclone season. Intense rainfall events (200+ mm/day) possible.
Flood Risk: HIGH — Cauvery delta region prone to severe flooding during heavy northeast monsoon.
Recommendations: Complete Samba paddy transplanting by August. Harvest Kuruvai paddy before October cyclone window. Ensure adequate crop insurance coverage.

ADVISORY: KARNATAKA — KHARIF
Districts: Dharwad, Shimoga, Gulbarga, Bellary
Expected Rainfall: 500-800 mm (varies significantly between Malnad and Maidan regions)
Drought Risk: MODERATE to HIGH — Northern Karnataka (Gulbarga, Bellary) is chronically drought-prone. Rainfed agriculture dominates.
Recommendations: Adopt drought-tolerant crop varieties. Use micro-irrigation (drip/sprinkler) under PMKSY scheme. For groundnut in Dharwad, ensure seed treatment with Trichoderma before sowing.

SECTION: RABI SEASON (November - March)
WEATHER PATTERN: Post-monsoon dry period. Western disturbances bring occasional winter rainfall to North India. South India depends on residual northeast monsoon moisture.
TEMPERATURE: Night temperatures drop to 5-10°C in central India, 0-5°C in North India. Frost risk in December-January in Punjab, Haryana, Rajasthan, and MP.
Recommendations: For wheat in North India, complete sowing by November 25 for optimal yields. Late sowing (after December 15) should use late-sown varieties. Apply first irrigation (CRI stage) at 21 days after sowing.

SECTION: SUMMER/ZAID SEASON (March - May)
WEATHER PATTERN: Hot and dry. Temperatures exceed 40°C in many regions. Heatwave conditions common in April-May.
WATER STRESS: Severe water scarcity in rainfed regions. Reservoir levels at lowest point.
Recommendations: Grow short-duration vegetables (okra, cucumber, watermelon) with drip irrigation. Mulching reduces soil temperature by 3-5°C and conserves moisture. Avoid transplanting during peak afternoon heat (12-3 PM).

SECTION: EXTREME WEATHER RISK MANAGEMENT
HEATWAVE: When temperature exceeds 40°C for 3+ consecutive days. Spray Kaolin (5%) on fruit crops to reduce heat stress. Irrigate during early morning or late evening. Provide shade nets for nurseries.
COLD WAVE/FROST: When minimum temperature drops below 4°C. Light irrigation in the evening raises field temperature by 1-2°C, protecting crops. Use straw mulch around base of fruit trees. Smoke screens in orchards (burning damp straw at field margins) raises ambient temperature.
HAILSTORM: Sudden, localized damage to standing crops. Immediately report damage to insurance company within 72 hours. Document damage with photographs. Apply foliar micronutrient spray to help recovery of damaged plants.
DROUGHT: Consecutive dry spells exceeding 21 days during crop growth. Activate alternate furrow irrigation to save 30-40% water. Apply potassium silicate foliar spray to improve drought tolerance. Consider contingency crop plan from NICRA (National Innovations in Climate Resilient Agriculture).
FLOOD: Drain waterlogged fields within 48 hours to prevent root rot. Apply lime @ 200 kg/ha in acidified waterlogged soils. Re-sow with short-duration varieties if crop loss exceeds 50%.
