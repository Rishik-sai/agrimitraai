SECTION: DEMAND AND EXPORT TRENDS
Paddy/Rice: DEMAND HIGH — India is the world's largest rice exporter. Basmati export demand strong from Middle East and EU markets.
Cotton: DEMAND MEDIUM — Domestic textile mills are operating at 75% capacity. Global cotton prices soft due to increased production in Brazil and USA.
Groundnut: DEMAND LOW to MEDIUM — Export demand subdued. Major buyer Indonesia has shifted partially to Malaysian palm oil substitutes.
Chillies: DEMAND HIGH — Strong export demand from Sri Lanka, Bangladesh, and Southeast Asian markets for Guntur Teja and 334 varieties.
Maize: DEMAND MEDIUM — Steady demand from poultry feed and starch industries. Ethanol blending program creating new demand channel.
Turmeric: DEMAND HIGH — Pharmaceutical and nutraceutical demand rising globally. Nizamabad turmeric commands premium pricing.
Soyabean: DEMAND MEDIUM — Crushing industry demand stable. International prices linked to CBOT futures.

SECTION: STORAGE AND POST-HARVEST MANAGEMENT
General Guidelines: Always dry produce to recommended moisture levels before storage. Use moisture meters for accurate readings.
Paddy: Store below 13.5% moisture. Use hermetic bags on elevated pallets. Fumigate with Aluminium Phosphide only under expert supervision.
Cotton: Store bales in dry ventilated sheds. Avoid moisture contact. Compress bales to standard density for transport.
Groundnut: Dry pods below 8% moisture. Store in clean gunny bags. Prevent Aflatoxin contamination by maintaining low humidity.
Chillies: Sun-dry to 10% moisture. Cold storage at 4-10°C preserves capsaicin and color for premium export pricing.
Maize: Maintain grain below 12% moisture. Fumigate bins before loading. Monitor regularly for grain weevils.

SECTION: MARKET ADVISORY
Farmers are advised to register on the e-NAM (National Agriculture Market) portal for transparent electronic bidding. PM-AASHA scheme provides price support operations when market prices fall below MSP. Farmers should obtain MSP receipts from government procurement centers. Direct Benefit Transfer (DBT) ensures payments directly to farmer bank accounts within 72 hours of procurement.

SECTION: MILLET MARKET SURGE (SHREE ANNA)
Bajra (Pearl Millet) and Ragi (Finger Millet): DEMAND VERY HIGH — Following the global push for millets, export demand to Europe and Middle East has surged by 40%. Domestic FMCG companies are heavily procuring millets for health-focused packaged foods. Farmers can expect prices consistently ₹300-₹500 above MSP in open markets.

SECTION: ORGANIC PREMIUM PRICING
Organic Produce: DEMAND HIGH — Certified organic crops command a significant price premium across APMC mandis and direct-to-buyer channels.
- Organic Cotton: +25% premium over conventional MSP.
- Organic Turmeric: +30-40% premium, especially for varieties with high curcumin content (>5%).
- Organic Pulses (Tur/Moong): +20% premium.
Farmers are advised to obtain PGS-India (Participatory Guarantee System) certification to access these premium rates.
